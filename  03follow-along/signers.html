Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some signers stand out

Thomas Jefferson
Primary author of the Declaration of Independence and later the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams
Member of the Committee of Five, key advocate for independence, and later the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Member of the Committee of Five, renowned diplomat, inventor, and the oldest signer at age 70.
John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress and the first signer, known for his large, bold signature.
Samuel Adams
Influential revolutionary leader and political organizer who helped drive the movement toward independence.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The last surviving signer of the Declaration, a prominent Maryland statesman and wealthy landowner.

The Declaration of Independence was signed by 56 men, but a handful—like Thomas Jefferson, John Adams, Benjamin Franklin, and John Hancock—became enduring symbols of the American Revolution. Their leadership, ideas, and public roles helped shape the nation's founding principles and ensured their place in history. While every signer contributed to the cause of independence, these figures stand out for their lasting influence on the United States' political and cultural identity.
