What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the practice of using technology safely,responsibly,ethically, and effectively in online spaces-at school,at work, and in everyday life.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law is one of the most practical—and often overlooked—parts of digital citizenship. It's all about understanding what is legal and illegal when using technology, and how our actions online have real-world consequences.

What is AUP?

An AUP, or Acceptable Use Policy, is a set of rules that explains how people are allowed to use technology, networks, and digital resources within an organization—like a school, business, or library.

What is Plgiarism?

Plagiarism is when someone uses another person's words, ideas, images, or work and presents them as their own without giving proper credit.

It's essentially a form of intellectual theft.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is a broad term for any action that gives a student an unfair academic advantage by breaking rules or misrepresenting their work.

It's essentially cheating in any form within an educational setting.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

AI-generated content is becoming a big part of school, work, and creative life, so most institutions have started setting clear expectations.

The exact rules depend on your teacher, school, or workplace, but there are some widely accepted principles that show up almost everywhere.
